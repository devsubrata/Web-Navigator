<p style='text-align: center; margin-top: 50px;'><a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/features/the-english-we-speak_2026/ep-260105" target="_blank" style="display: inline-block; text-decoration: none; color: darkmagenta; font-weight: bold; font-size: 35px; padding: 5px 20px; border: 2px solid blue; border-radius: 10px;">Lock in</a></p>
Episode 260105 / 05 Jan 2026<br><br>
<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 22px; display: block;'>Lock in</span>
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '> ➜ focus really hard on something, commit to the tasks and avoid distractions; <br> ➜ "Lock in" is all about intense focus, determination and boosting productivity.</span> 
<hr style="background: #fe3b00; height: 4px">
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★ I am exhausted. I've been studying for hours before my Spanish exam. I really had to lock in last night. </span> 
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★ You might also hear "lock in" about sports. Players might lock in before they play a big match.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★ We can lock in on our goals. I'm preparing to run a half marathon in three months, so I need to lock in on my fitness goals.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★ I really need to stop failing my driving test. I'm going to just lock in and do loads of practice.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★ Right guys. I've done no work all day, so I've had a coffee, and this afternoon I really just need to lock in and get some stuff done.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★ I never see my kids in the evening anymore. They're locking in for their exams.</span>

<p style='text-align: center; margin-top: 50px;'><a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/features/the-english-we-speak_2026/ep-260112" target="_blank" style="display: inline-block; text-decoration: none; color: darkmagenta; font-weight: bold; font-size: 35px; padding: 5px 20px; border: 2px solid blue; border-radius: 10px;">Aura farming</a></p>
Episode 260112 / 12 Jan 2026<br><br>
<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 22px; display: block;'>Aura farming</span>
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '>➜ do something for attention of praise.</span> 
<hr style="background: #fe3b00; height: 4px">
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ That guy's always carrying a skateboard. He doesn't even know how to use it. He's just aura farming.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ I went to this party – you won't believe this. There were a couple of people there in the dark wearing sunglasses. I mean, stop aura farming, guys.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ I have this friend that always poses next to sports cars and then posts them on social media, like the cars are his. He's just aura farming. So embarrassing.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ A friend of mine loves going to fancy and expensive restaurants and then posting pictures of them. She's definitely aura farming.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ I think my brother was trying to earn 'aura points' on holiday. He kept looking out at the sea wearing his new cool sunglasses and asking me to take pictures of him.</span>

<p style='text-align: center; margin-top: 50px;'><a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/features/the-english-we-speak_2026/ep-260119" target="_blank" style="display: inline-block; text-decoration: none; color: darkmagenta; font-weight: bold; font-size: 35px; padding: 5px 20px; border: 2px solid blue; border-radius: 10px;">Hot on the heels</a></p>
Episode 260119 / 19 Jan 2026<br><br>
<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 22px; display: block;'>Hot on the heels</span> 
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '>
➜ something happens immediately after something else, often unexpectedly.<br>
➜ We also use 'hot on the heels' to talk about following closely behind someone or something, especially if you're likely to succeed in what you're aiming to do.<br>
➜ Variations of this phrase include 'hot on the tail' or 'hot on the trail'.<br>
</span> 
<hr style="background: #fe3b00; height: 4px; border: none;">
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ My cousin won a cooking competition last week and hot on the heels of that she's been asked to cater for a wedding.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ One of my favourite singers just released a new album, and her new world tour announcement came hot on the heels of the album's release.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ There's another storm coming tonight. Hot on the heels of the flooding yesterday.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ Hot on the heels of my massive pay rise, I won the lottery!</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ Hot on the heels of their league win, the team then won the cup.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ The police car was hot on the heels of the robber.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦  In a running race, the person in second might be hot on the heels of the person in front. They are very close to beating them.</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;✦ I watched a film last night where detectives were hot on the tail of a con man.</span>

<p style='text-align: center; margin-top: 50px;'><a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/features/the-english-we-speak_2026/ep-260126" target="_blank" style="display: inline-block; text-decoration: none; color: darkmagenta; font-weight: bold; font-size: 35px; padding: 5px 20px; border: 2px solid blue; border-radius: 10px;">Shoot off</a></p>
Episode Episode 260126 / 26 Jan 2026<br><br>
<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 25px; display: block;'>Shoot off</span>  
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '> 
➜ This expression means leave quickly because you're in a hurry or there's somewhere you need to be.  <br> 
➜ Hi Feifei. Let's do this quickly. I need to shoot off right after the recording. <br> 
➜ We can't rush! This script is important. 'Shoot off' – this means to leave quickly because you're in a hurry or there's somewhere you need to be. <br>  
➜ So 'shoot off' means to leave a place quickly because you need to be somewhere else.<br>
➜ A similar expression is 'dash off'. We can also say 'head out' but this doesn't have the same sense of urgency as 'shoot off' or 'dash off'.<br>
</span> 
<hr style="background: #fe3b00; height: 3px; border: none;">
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I will need to shoot off because I have a dentist appointment, and I can’t be late again. </span> 
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  If you need to leave early, you might warn people, "sorry, but I have to shoot off after this". </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I told everyone today I will have to shoot off straight after the meeting tomorrow to catch my train. </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★   Are we done? I need to shoot off!</span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I always have to shoot off quickly from work because I don't want to miss my train home. </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  Sorry, I've just got to shoot off. My mum's calling me. </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  Sorry team, got to shoot off. I need to pick the kids up! </span>

<p style='text-align: center; margin-top: 50px;'><a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/features/the-english-we-speak_2026/ep-260202" target="_blank" style="display: inline-block; text-decoration: none; color: darkmagenta; font-weight: bold; font-size: 35px; padding: 5px 20px; border: 2px solid blue; border-radius: 10px;">Facepalm moment</a></p>
Episode 260202 / 02 Feb 2026<br><br>
<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 22px; display: block;'>Facepalm moment</span> 
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '> 
➜ A facepalm is where you have your head against the palm of your hand because you're embarrassed, annoyed or frustrated. Something that makes you do that, is a facepalm moment.<br>  
➜ The facepalm moment is when you realise that you've done something wrong or silly. Sometimes you might do it in response to someone else doing something wrong or silly. 
</span> 
<hr style="background: #fe3b00; height: 4px">
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I decided to cycle to work today, I usually bring some clothes to change into when I get to work, but today I got here, looked in my bag and saw that I'd left my trousers at home – it was a real facepalm moment! </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I travelled two hours to take photos of this amazing sunset and then I realised I forgot my camera. What a facepalm moment! </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  Someone was waving at me, so I waved back and then I realised they were waving at someone else. It was a bit of a facepalm moment. </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I had a facepalm moment today when I was sending a very detailed voice note to my mum and then I realised I hadn't pressed record. </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I facepalmed so hard when I realised I'd left my trousers at home. </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I was certainly facepalming very hard when you told me that you'd forgotten your trousers. </span>

<p style='text-align: center; margin-top: 50px;'><a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/features/the-english-we-speak_2026/ep-260209" target="_blank" style="display: inline-block; text-decoration: none; color: darkmagenta; font-weight: bold; font-size: 35px; padding: 5px 20px; border: 2px solid blue; border-radius: 10px;">A big ask</a></p>
Episode 260209 / 09 Feb 2026<br><br>
<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 25px; display: block;'>A big ask</span>  
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '> ➜ If you ask someone to do something difficult, it's a big ask. <br>  ➜ a big ask is a request for something that you know will be difficult for the person that you are asking. </span> 
<hr style="background: #fe3b00; height: 4px">
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  You know you were going to take next week off work, and I was going to take the week after that off. It turns out I mixed up the dates – I booked flights for next week. Could I swap weeks with you and take next week off? I know it's a big ask. </span> 
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  Phil, that is a big ask. I don’t think I can do that – I've already got everything planned out. </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  Asking me to do anything before I've had my morning coffee is a big ask. </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I know it's a big ask, but can you pick me up from the airport next weekend. My flight gets in at 3am. </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  To start with, my neighbour asked me to water his plants while he's on holiday, but now he's added mowing the lawn and that's a big ask. </span>

<p style='text-align: center; margin-top: 50px;'><a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/features/the-english-we-speak_2026/ep-260216" target="_blank" style="display: inline-block; text-decoration: none; color: darkmagenta; font-weight: bold; font-size: 35px; padding: 5px 20px; border: 2px solid blue; border-radius: 10px;">Learn the hard way</a></p>
Episode 260216 / 16 Feb 2026<br><br>
<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 25px; display: block;'>Learn the hard way</span>  
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '> 
➜ If you learn something the hard way, it's because you weren’t listening or you made a mistake.<br> 
➜ The weather seemed fine when I left home, but then I got five minutes down the road, and it started raining really heavily. I guess I learnt the hard way that you need to have an umbrella if you live in London! <br> 
➜ Learning something the hard way is a bit like learning from your mistakes. It means something difficult or unpleasant happened to you, so you learn to do something differently to make sure it doesn't happen again. <br>  
➜ If you lost all your work because you forgot to save, you'll learn why saving is important the hard way, when your computer crashes. <br>
➜ It's often used to suggest that someone has ignored advice. <br>
➜ the expression 'learn something the hard way' to mean learning from bad experiences. <br> 
➜ A similar expression we can say that sometimes people have to learn hard lessons. If we say someone has to learn hard lessons, we mean that they are doing something wrong and they need to learn from the consequences. <br> 
</span> 
<hr style="background: #fe3b00; height: 3px; border: none;">
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I didn't use to look at the cookbooks when I cook, but then I did because I learned the hard way and cooked so many terrible dishes. </span> 
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  I was getting the train to Paris from London, and I learned the hard way that you need an hour before the train. I only arrived 15 minutes before the train, so I missed it. </span>
<span style='display: block;'>&ensp;&ensp;★  People say when you go travelling, you should take half of what you think you should take, and I didn’t take that advice so I learnt the hard way. I carried a really heavy bag around with me all the time for no reason. </span>
