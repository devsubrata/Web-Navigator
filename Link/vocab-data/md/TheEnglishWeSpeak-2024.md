Episode 240102 / 02 Jan 2024<br>
<audio controls>

<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 25px; display: block;'>Bring to the party / Bring something to the table</span>  
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '> 
➜ You want to say that you can offer a lot. <br> 
➜ have something good to offer in a situation <br> 
➜ when we say 'bring something to the party', 'bring something' means 'offer something' and 'the party' means a situation. <br>  
➜ the expression 'bring something to the party', which is a way of saying you contribute something positive. 
</span> 
<hr style="background:#03DAC5; height: 3px; border: none;">
<span style='display: block; padding-left: 25px;'>
	★  You have the qualifications for the job, but what other skills can you bring to the party? <br>
	★  Her investment will bring a lot to the party. She's such a big name in business. <br>
	★  I might be young, but I think I bring a lot to the party. <br>
	★  I've been very busy preparing. I’ve been working here for many years, so I'll be bringing lots of expertise to the party. <br>
	★  As long as you bring creativity to the party too, we'll have another exciting year of English programmes. <br>
	★  Beth, you're coming to the big new year meeting too, aren't you? I am but you'll have to wait to see what I'm bringing to the table! <br>
</span> 

<p style='text-align: center; margin-top: 50px;'><a href="https://www.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/english/features/the-english-we-speak_2024/ep-240108" target="_blank" style="display: inline-block; text-decoration: none; color: darkmagenta; font-weight: bold; font-size: 35px; padding: 5px 20px; border: 2px solid blue; border-radius: 10px;">Elbow grease</a></p>
Episode 240108 / 08 Jan 2024<br>
<audio controls>
	<source src="https://downloads.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/features/tews/240109_tews_elbow_grease_download.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">

<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 25px; display: block;'>Elbow grease</span>  
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '> 
➜ a phrase that describes honest hard physical work. <br> 
➜ the expression ‘elbow grease’, which means ‘hard, usually physical work’ 
</span> 
<hr style="background: #03DAC5; height: 3px; border: none;">
<span style='display: block; padding-left: 25px;'>
	★  The kitchen looks sparkling now, but it took a lot of elbow grease to get there! <br>
	★  The sports teams are only successful because of the elbow grease put in by the volunteers, coaches and organisers. <br>
	★  You call those shoes polished? You need a bit more elbow grease than that! <br>
	★  My car is absolutely filthy. I need to get it clean – it’s hard work and there are some bits that won’t be easy to clean, but with a bit of elbow grease, I can get it done <br>
</span>

Episode 240115 / 15 Jan 2024<br>
<audio controls>

<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 25px; display: block;'>Rears its head / 'rear its ugly head'</span>  
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '> 
➜ an expression which is about a bad thing appearing or starting to happen.<br> 
</span> 
<hr style="background: #03DAC5; height: 3px; border: none;">
<span style='display: block; padding-left: 25px;'>
	★  Attendance on the course was good until colds and flu reared their heads in the winter. <br>
	★  I was working outside until the wet weather reared its head and I had to go inside. <br>
	★  Food prices were going down, until drought reared its ugly head. <br>
	★  I bought a new computer last week! I can't get it to work properly. The speakers don't work, it has a virus. Every time I think I've fixed it, a new problem rears its head!<br>
	★ I think the end of this programme is rearing its ugly head now. So, we have to end here.
</span>

Episode 240122 / 22 Jan 2024<br>
<audio controls>
	<source src="https://downloads.bbc.co.uk/learningenglish/features/tews/240122_tews_a_blow_download.mp3" type="audio/mpeg">

<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 25px; display: block;'>A blow</span>  
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '> 
➜ An expression to say something is disappointing. <br>  
➜ What a blow! This word is especially used when something hasn't gone the way you imagined and has ruined your plans.<br>
➜ And it's often used when something is delayed, cancelled or fails. <br>
➜ the word 'blow' in 'a blow', which is a way of saying something has happened that is disappointing.
</span> 
<hr style="background: #03DAC5; height: 3px; border: none;">
<span style='display: block; padding-left: 25px;'>
	★  We didn’t get the funding so the project can’t go ahead. It’s a blow. <br>
	★  It was a bit of a blow when they found out the festival was cancelled. <br>
	★  We’d made a lunch reservation for 1pm so it was a blow when the train was delayed until 1.30. <br>
	★  I've been trying to buy a new car. I saw one I really liked, and I was just about to buy it, but I've just found out that it sold yesterday. It's a bit of a blow. <br>
	★  Did you find your phone by the way? I heard you lost it. No. I've lost all my pictures and contacts. It's a blow.<br>
	★  By the way, Neil is selling a car. Did you know? Yes. It's a nice one, but far too expensive for me! Another blow. Oh well. Good luck finding one. 
</span>

Episode 240129 / 29 Jan 2024<br>
<audio controls>

<span style='color: brown; font-weight: bold; font-size: 25px; display: block;'>On the go</span>  
<span style='font-weight: bold; color: indigo; '> 
➜ If you're always busy, you're always 'on the go'.  <br>  
➜ an expression that means you're busy and active. It can be positive or negative. <br>
➜ 'on the go' means busy, active and not relaxing. <br>
➜ this phrase is very commonly used with adverbs of frequency, like 'always', 'usually' and 'never'.
</span> 
<hr style="background: #03DAC5; height: 3px; border: none;">
<span style='display: block; padding-left: 25px;'>
	★  Calum is away for work again. He’s always on the go.<br>
	★  I’m usually on the go early, so it doesn’t matter what time you arrive, even if it’s before breakfast!<br>
	★  She’s so tired. She’s always on the go since starting her new job.<br>
	★  You're always busy Feifei, and you're looking a bit tired if you don't mind me saying so, but it's no surprise because you are always on the go. <br>
	★  Well, honestly, it's quite difficult having to balance work, children and friends. I'm always on the go! <br>
	★  I could say I'm always on the go during the week because of work, school pickups and other activities, but at the weekends I'm usually not on the go because that's when I try and relax.
<br>
</span>
